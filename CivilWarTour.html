Civil War in the Wild West Tour!

Though not as significant as the campaigns in northern Virginia and on the Mississippi, the battles between the Union and the Confederacy in the western United States had resounding implications for the entire war!

The CSA dreamt of a transcontinental empire of sorts, they sought to create one during the war.





Day 1:

You get to see the largest battle that occured in the far West, the battle of Glorieta Pass.

This battle was a crucial Union victory because it stopped a Confederate army into the West. The Confederates zealously guarded the pass for three days until they were forced to retreat when the saw their poorly guarded supply wagons destroyed by union forces who went around rough terrain to reach.
